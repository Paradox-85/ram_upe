From AI whisperer chat

Where Ramboll Tech can help is in establishing logical and physical architectures that facilitate interrogation by AI models.  The

challenge I see with data structures is that it is a matter of organization more than technology.

 To understand how chemical hazard documents, architectural codes and design best practices must be organized to facilitate AI-

assistance in determining design requirements for a specific factory, one must understand the analytical, creative, risk and

decision making processes inherent in performing that workflow.  Same could be said for the analysis performed by an

environmental engineer to identify potentially responsible parties (PRPs) for a polluted watershed.  The combination of public

records, internal reports, GIS data, corporate history and lab test results (among others) poses a challenge for AI information

gathering and synthesis particularly as it relates to the steps required to perform that task.

 For Ramboll Tech to develop a centralized strategy for organizing data/information would require omniscient understanding of

the work we do across Ramboll's GBAs.  It would be a heavy lift even for our small IDT organizations bound to the GBAs.  This is

why I believe our focus must be on pushing GBA leaders to take ownership for this (information organization/structuring) task.

The long term performance dividend AI is going to afford our P&Ls will justify the near term effort.

 Ramboll Tech will be a critical expert resource to these domain experts in determining what cloud structures are most efficient,

how information can be encoded for easier AI model access and how to normalize information to avoid an explosion of

duplicative entries as each domain seeks to develop their own approach.

That's how I've been thinking about this information structuring challenge.  How does this align with your view?

Terms and Definitions

1. What is UPE?

The Unified Production Environment (UPE) is Ramboll’s global digital backbone.

It is designed to create a seamless, unified digital workspace for engineers and a

professional interface for client interaction.

The core philosophy is the "Parameterized Constructor" (similar to a modern

automotive platform):

•  The Core: A stable, standardized set of central components and data

structures (the "chassis").

•  The Variation: A library of modular, typical solutions that can be combined

and adapted to create thousands of unique, high-quality design projects.

•  The Output: An intelligent 3D model linked to a structured database and

automated documentation.

The Unified Production Environment (UPE) is not just a software application; it is

a flexible, API-first framework designed to integrate Ramboll’s engineering DNA

into every project. It serves as a "Digital Asset Hub" that captures knowledge

from fragmented sources—CAD models, documents, and databases—and

transforms them into reusable, standardized assets.

The "Car Platform" Analogy: UPE provides the "Chassis" (the core API, security,

and data standards). On top of this, engineers assemble "Modular Blocks"

(standardized engineering solutions) to build a "Unique Vehicle" (the specific

design project) that is high-quality, parametric, and data-driven.
